CORPUS
ABOUT

I am a self-taught photographer working at the intersection of the visible and the felt. By day I work in computer vision — training machines to see. By night, or in the quiet hours between, I make photographs that try to do something machines cannot: to produce awe.

CORPUS is a project crystalizing slowly through many years — the oldest photographs are from around 2015. It grew from a long fascination with the body as both biological fact and philosophical question — with skin, bone, how they emerge from inanimate matter and how it later allow emotion and higher thought emerge from it to try give it direction and meaning.

The nudes in this work are photographs of myself, my partner, and friends who trusted me with their image. I am a feminist, and that shapes how I work: no one here is an object. Everyone chose to be seen.

I am drawn to subjects that make me feel small — glaciers, fossils, skulls, feathers, skin at close range. I look for the same feeling in other people's art. If something in these images stops you for a moment longer than you expected, that is exactly what I was after.

Marcin Łapiński
